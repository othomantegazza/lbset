First: download Rstudio

Notes - The RStudio IDE

Open Rstudio:

  • Even if you explore your data interactively, Always Open use a script! To keep track of what you are doing
  • Send commands to the console with Command + Return
  • Check the objects in your environment on the upper right side
  • The fourth panel is just for fun (plots, web viewers)

Most of the packages you need are in the Tidyverse

Notes - Get data into R

  • Use readr to read rectangular "text" data.
  • Use readxl to read data from Excel.
  • Use JSONlite

Notes - Get data into R

Notes - Manipulate Data with DPLYR and PURRR

DPLYR has a declarative syntax ("inspired" to SQL): You don't tell your script how to manipulate your data, you tell it what you want to achieve!

PURRR contains tools for functional programming, you will need it, especially for looping.

Notes: Visualize your data with ggplot2

ggplot2 is a high level plot library!

It's planned so you can make plots quickly from data with good defaults and minimal customization.

Though you can customize them as much as you want.
